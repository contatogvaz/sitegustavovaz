Gustavo Vaz is a Brazilian actor, author, and multimedia creator. His artistic investigation articulates presence, mediation, and experience, while challenging theatrical language in dialogue with technological and territorial contexts. Alongside a consistent career as an actor, he has developed a distinct authorial path through works that explore site-specific dramaturgies, sound immersions, digital structures, and the active role of the audience in the construction of scenic experience.

Authorial Practice

ExCompanhia and the authorial field

Over more than a decade, Gustavo Vaz has built a body of work in which theatre, sound, urban space, digital devices, and mediated presence operate as formal tools rather than ornament. The research is not about technology as theme, but about how contemporary systems of mediation reorganize perception, listening, presence, and experience.

Why theatre matters here

The stage trajectory remains essential, but it is framed here as part of a larger authorial field. The acting work gives force, precision, and public visibility to the artistic path; the authorial research gives singularity, coherence, and future direction.

This balance matters: Gustavo Vaz is not presented as an actor with side projects, but as an artist whose acting career, writing, direction, and research reinforce one another.
